APPEAL CASE DOCUMENT

Case Title: Rajesh Kumar vs State of Delhi
Court: Sessions Court, Delhi
Case Type: Criminal Appeal

Facts of the Case:
The appellant, Rajesh Kumar, was convicted under Section 420 IPC for cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property. The prosecution alleged that the appellant misrepresented himself as a government official and collected money from multiple victims promising government jobs.

Grounds of Appeal:

1. The trial court failed to properly evaluate the evidence presented by the defense.
2. There was no clear proof of dishonest intention at the time of the alleged act.
3. The witnesses presented by the prosecution gave inconsistent statements.
4. The appellant was falsely implicated due to personal rivalry.

Arguments by Appellant:
The appellant argues that the money received was part of a private consultancy service and not a fraudulent act. There was no intention to cheat, and all transactions were done with consent.

Arguments by Respondent (State):
The prosecution maintains that the accused deliberately deceived victims and collected money under false pretenses, which clearly establishes the offence under Section 420 IPC.

Relief Sought:
The appellant requests the court to set aside the conviction and acquit him of all charges.

Conclusion:
The appeal is filed on the grounds of improper appreciation of evidence and lack of criminal intent.
